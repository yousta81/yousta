Mercury has a unique spin-orbit resonance, completing about 3 rotations for every 2 orbits around the Sun.
Mercury's mass is about 0.055 times that of Earth, but it's diameter is approximately 0.3825 smaller.
Venus Venus has a scorching surface temperature of up to 467 degrees Celsius due to a thick atmosphere mostly of carbon dioxide.
Venus rotates slowly (243 Earth days) in a retrograde direction, unique among planets.
Earth Earth is the only known planet to support life.
Planet has a dynamic and active geology, with processes like plate tectonics shaping its surface.
Earth's magnetosphere deflects and traps solar wind, preventing it from directly hitting the Earth's atmosphere.
The Moon plays a role in stabilizing Earth's axial tilt, which contributes to the planet's relatively stable climate over long periods.
Mars "Red Planet's" reddish appearance is due to iron oxide (rust) on its surface.
Mars is home to the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons (22 kilometers high).
The average surface temperature on Mars around minus 62 degrees Celsius.
Mars has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos.
Jupiter Jupiter is a gas giant making up about 70% of the total mass of all the planets in our solar system.
It boasts a powerful magnetic field, over ten times stronger than any other planet in our solar system.
Jupiter's iconic Great Red Spot is a massive storm that has been raging for at least 350 years.
Saturn Saturn is renowned for its stunning ring system, which consists of countless icy particles and debris, that extend outward for thousands of kilometers.
Saturn is a gas giant predominantly composed of hydrogen and helium.
Saturn's fascinating notable feature is the hexagonal storm at its north pole. Each side of the hexagon is about 13,800 kilometers long.
Uranus Uranus essentially rolling around the Sun on its side, with its axis of rotation almost parallel to its orbital plane.
The planet's atmosphere is composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and methane, giving it a bluish-green hue, and it has a set of thin, faint rings surrounding it.
Uranus is considered the coldest planet in our solar system
Neptune One year on Neptune is equivalent to about 165 Earth years.
Neptune was the first planet to be discovered through mathematical predictions.
Neptune has the fastest winds in the solar system, with speeds that can reach up to 1,930 kilometers per hour.
